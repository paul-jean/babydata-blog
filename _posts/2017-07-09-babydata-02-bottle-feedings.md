This is a powerful visualization showing how Toby\[CloseCurlyQuote]s feedings have organized over time. We\[CloseCurlyQuote]ve made an effort to get him onto a schedule of eating every 4 hours during the day, which shows up clearly as bands of feedings happening at about 6 AM, 10 AM, 2 PM, and 6 PM. There have been several feedings at night between midnight and 5 AM as well, but these have reduced over time. The sleep training program we\[CloseCurlyQuote]re following starts with getting the baby on a schedule of eating every 4 hours, then gradually reducing the amount they are fed at night. Once the night feedings have been eliminated, the baby is hopefully no longer hungry at night, and can be soothed back to sleep without food when they wake up at night out of habit. The night wakings can then hopefully be eliminated altogether.

We\[CloseCurlyQuote]ve wondered if the amount Toby eats at night (12 AM to 5 AM) is at all related to the amount he has eaten during the previous day (5 AM to 12 AM the previous day). Or more specifically, whether it depends on the amount he ate at his last feeding of the day around 6 PM. We thought he might be \[OpenCurlyDoubleQuote]filling up the tank\[CloseCurlyDoubleQuote] to get him through the night in his last feeding at 6 PM, and if he didn\[CloseCurlyQuote]t eat enough he would wake up sooner at night because he runs out of calories. But we\[CloseCurlyQuote]ve also read that his sleep isn\[CloseCurlyQuote]t really influenced by how much he ate in his last feeding (after about 6 weeks of age).
